2. A spark of static electricity can measure up to 3,000 volts.

3. Lightning is a discharge of electricity in the atmosphere. Lightning bolts can travel at around 130,000 miles per hour and reach nearly 54,000 °F in temperature.

Electricity Conservation Tips

-
  • Turn off lights and appliances when not in use.
  • Use energy-efficient LED bulbs.
  • Unplug chargers and electronics when not in use.
  • Optimize air conditioner usage.

    • Some government policies on electricity conservation are:

  • The Energy Conservation Act 2001, which provides the legal framework and institutional arrangement for energy efficiency and conservation. The Act empowers the Bureau of Energy Efficiency (BEE) to implement various programs and schemes for energy efficiency and conservation, such as standards and labeling, energy conservation building codes, energy audits, demand side management, etc

  • The National Mission for Enhanced Energy Efficiency (NMEEE), which is one of the eight missions under the National Action Plan on Climate Change (NAPCC). The mission aims to achieve energy savings of 23 million tons of oil equivalent by 2022 through four initiatives: Perform Achieve and Trade (PAT), Market Transformation for Energy Efficiency (MTEE), Energy Efficiency Financing Platform (EEFP), and Framework for Energy Efficient Economic Development (FEEED)

  • The UJALA scheme, which stands for Unnat Jyoti by Affordable LEDs for All. The scheme aims to distribute LED bulbs to domestic consumers at subsidized rates, and replace incandescent bulbs and CFLs with LED bulbs. The scheme is expected to save about 40 billion units of electricity per year and reduce CO2 emissions by 20 million tons per year.

  • The Star Rating Programme, which is a voluntary scheme for buildings that rates them on a scale of 1 to 5 stars based on their actual performance in terms of energy use. The scheme covers commercial and residential buildings, and helps consumers to make informed choices about energy efficiency and conservation.
